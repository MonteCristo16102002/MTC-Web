The UEFA Champions League is an annual club football tournament organized by the European Football Federation (UEFA) for the top-ranked clubs in the European national championships. It is one of the most prestigious football competitions in the world and the most prestigious club competition in European football, including the champions of national championships (for some countries also have one or more runner-up teams) of national associations.Introduced in 1955 as the European Champion Clubs' Cup, the tournament was originally played in a knockout format and was open to the competition only. champion clubs of each national championship. The tournament took its current name in 1992, added a two-leg round-robin group stage and allowed multiple clubs from the same country to participate. The tournament has since expanded, and while some of Europe's national championships can still only allow their champions to participate, the top leagues are now allowed to send up to four teams. Immediately following national championship clubs that did not qualify for the Champions League are allowed to participate in the UEFA Europa League second division, and from 2021, teams that did not qualify for the UEFA Europa League will qualify for the UEFA Europa League. a new third tier league called the UEFA Europa Conference League
